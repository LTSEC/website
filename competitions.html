Competitions

Collegiate Cyber Defense Competition (CCDC)

The Southwest Collegiate Cybersecurity Defense Competition (SWCCDC) is an annual cybersecurity collegiate competition, which is a part of the National CCDC circuit. The winning team of SWCCDC goes on to compete at National CCDC, one of the most prestigous cybersecurity competitions in the country.

The LTSEC CCDC team is the flagship competitive cyber team for Louisiana Tech and its most competitive.

LTSEC has competed in SWCCDC since 2020, and has qualified for every SWCCDC main event since competing.

Collegiate Penetration Testing Competition (CPTC)

The Collegiate Penetration Testing Competition (CPTC) focuses on training and evaluating the activities teams are likely to perform during a real-world penetration testing engagement. Instead of defending your network, searching for flags, or claiming ownership of systems, CPTC focuses on mimicking the activities performed during a real-world penetration testing engagement conducted by companies, professional services firms, and internal security departments around the world.

LTSEC is currently vying to compete in the 2023-24 season of CPTC.

Louisiana Tech Cyber Storm

Cyber Storm is Louisiana Tech's annual cybersecurity project-based class, available to Computer Science and Cyber Engineering students annually. Cyber Storm participants learn to utilize and interact with various parts of the cybersecurity realm, including introductions to transfer protocols, cryptography, and reverse engineering.

DoE CyberForce

The DoE CyberForce Program is a workforce development program run by Argonne National Laboratory and sponsored by the Department of Energy. The DoE CyberForce program notably includes the DoE CyberForce Competition, an annual hybrid cybersecurity competition that prepares students for building and defending the digital components of operational technology (OT) in the energy sector.

NCAE Cyber Games

The NCAE Cyber Games is an entry-level cybersecurity available exclusively to National Centers of Academic Excellence in Cybersecurity, including Louisiana Tech University. This competition is available only to students who have never participated in a collegiate cybersecurity event before, providing a welcoming and accessible introduction to cybersecurity for students of all ages, including mentoring from upperclassmen and tenured cybersecurity staff members.

National Cyber League (NCL)

The National Cyber League (NCL) is a quarterly collegiate cybersecurity competition, hosted online featuring a large variety of cybersecurity-based puzzles, including reverse engineering, open-source intelligence (OSINT), log analysis, and network analysis.

Hivestorm

Hivestorm is an annual remote competition run by the UTSA CIAS. Teams are given four (4) hours to secure provided Windows and Linux-based virtual machines – removing malware and other infections, correcting misconfigurations, mitigating vulnerabilities, disabling vulnerable services, and so on.

LTSEC CTF

The LTSEC CTF was an online asynchronous CTF competition created by LTSEC for the students of Louisiana Tech University to help facilitate interest in competitive CTFs and help introduce students to advanced concepts.

After a successful run in the 2022-23 academic year, LTSEC CTF was retired to focus efforts towards lectures, CCDC, and CPTC. The LTSEC CTF archive will be made available in the future, featuring all of the challenges created for the platform.
