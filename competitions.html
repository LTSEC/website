Cybersecurity Competitions

The Louisiana Tech Cybersecurity Defense Club participates in a number of national cyber competitions as a part of our program.

Cyber Storm

Cyber Storm is Louisiana Tech's annual cybersecurity project-based class, available to Computer Science and Cyber Engineering students annually. Cyber Storm participants learn to utilize and interact with various parts of the cybersecurity realm, including introductions to transfer protocols, cryptography, and reverse engineering.

NCAE Cyber Games

The NCAE Cyber Games is an entry-level cybersecurity available exclusively to National Centers of Academic Excellence in Cybersecurity, including Louisiana Tech University. This competition is available only to students who have never participated in a collegiate cybersecurity event before, providing a welcoming and accessible introduction to cybersecurity for students of all ages, including mentoring from upperclassmen and tenured cybersecurity staff members.

National Cyber League (NCL)

The National Cyber League (NCL) is a quarterly collegiate cybersecurity competition, hosted online featuring a large variety of cybersecurity-based puzzles, including reverse engineering, open-source intelligence (OSINT), log analysis, and network analysis.

Southwest Collegiate Cybersecurity Defense Competition (SWCCDC)

The Southwest Collegiate Cybersecurity Defense Competition (SWCCDC) is an annual cybersecurity collegiate competition, which is a part of the National CCDC circuit. The winning team of SWCCDC goes on to compete at National CCDC, one of the most prestigous cybersecurity competitions in the country.
